Voto Serif GX

History of the font

Voto Serif GX is a 3-axis variable font based on the existing font,  “Noto Serif”. Voto Serif GX was designed and published by creator Adam Twardoch, and was originally released in November 2016. Voto Serif GX’s broad range of glyphs includes multiple alphabets, and allows it to display type in several different languages. Along with the Latin alphabet, this font includes Greek, Cryillic, and Vietnamese alphabets. This font’s versatility also applies to its 567 text styles and its enormous number of glyphs ranging over 3,000. Voto Serif GX also includes numbers 0-9 as well as allowing for italics in any language or style. Voto Serif GX allows for weight and width as well as an optical size slider, which is currently still in progress.
